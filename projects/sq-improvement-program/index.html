Project Description

SQ's theoretical background is based on Harvard's Howard Earl Gardner's theory of multiple intelligences. Howard Gardner advocates 8 types of multiple intelligence and says that there are also additional SQs. Later, Professor Robert Emmons (Ph. D) from California State University made a significant contribution to the formation of the theoretical foundation of SQ, based on Howard Gardner's theory of multiple intelligence at Harvard University. Similarly, he argued that the ability to apply and use emotions in our lives is emotional intelligence, spiritual intelligence is also the mental ability to apply and use the spirituality inherent in all people in our lives, and has formed the theoretical basis of the SQ. Since then, SQ has been studied by various scien- tists, undergoes many academic discussions, and is recog- nized as a unique intelligence that distinguishes it from other objects belonging to humans.

Main Features

The SQ program was developed by the Global SQ Research Institute to help develop the right character, career guidance and potential, and it is an educational program consisting of SQ test and SQ improvement.

SQ Test: This measures the SQ using the G-SIT General-Spiritual Intelligence Test, an SQ measurement test paper developed by this institute. G-SIT is a test with high reliability and validity, and is the best and most reliable test in which all SQ studies around the world are integrated.

SQ Improvement: This is an education program for SQ improvement developed by the Global SQ Research Center by synthesizing and analyzing SQ theory and SQ research conducted around the world.
